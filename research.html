Research

I study how intraseasonal disturbances modulate rainfall, convection and extremes across the Indian region and the wider Indo-Pacific. Below is a quick primer and how it connects to my work.

BSISO basics

The Boreal Summer Intraseasonal Oscillation is a leading summer mode over the Asian monsoon, featuring northward propagation over the Indian Ocean and WNP and an equatorial eastward component. It shapes active/break spells and monsoon onset/progression.

Refs: JMSJ/NOAA review; Lee et al. (2013) indices.

MJO & S2S predictability

The MJO—an eastward-moving convective envelope—provides a backbone of subseasonal predictability, influencing monsoon variability and extremes.

Refs: Kim et al. 2018; NOAA explainer.

Toolbox & datasets

  • Reanalysis: ERA5; IMDAA (regional reanalysis by NCMRWF/partners).
  • Aerosol–cloud: CAMS for humidity/vertical velocity/aerosols.
  • Models: UK Met Office Unified Model (UM) for high‑res case studies.

Refs: IMDAA at NCMRWF.
